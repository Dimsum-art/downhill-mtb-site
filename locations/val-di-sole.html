Val di Sole's Black Snake track is perhaps the most feared course on the World Cup circuit. Notorious for its root-infested, punishing terrain that chews up bikes and riders alike. The Trentino valley setting is stunning -- dense forests, dramatic elevation changes, and unpredictable weather that can turn a dry run into a mud bath in minutes.

Beyond the race track, the bike park offers progressive trails through alpine meadows. But the roots here are legendary -- they don't forgive, and they don't negotiate. Every section of the Black Snake demands total concentration, precise weight distribution, and the kind of grip that only comes from running the widest tires your frame will clear.

The Trentino valley rewards those who look beyond the race track. Long descents through Val Meledrio, panoramic rides above the treeline, and some of the best mountain cuisine in Italy waiting at the bottom. Val di Sole is a place that tests you on the bike and rewards you off it.

"The roots at Val di Sole don't forgive. They don't even negotiate."

TRAIL HIGHLIGHTS

Black Snake DH

Expert

2.4km • 600m vertical drop

The World Cup course that breaks bikes and spirits. An unrelenting gauntlet of roots, rocks, and off-camber sections through dark forest. Wet conditions amplify the difficulty tenfold.

Val Meledrio Trail

Advanced

5.5km • 700m vertical drop

A long, natural trail descending through the Meledrio valley. Technical root sections mixed with fast open stretches. The length alone makes this a serious physical challenge.

Daolasa Flow

Intermediate

3.8km • 400m vertical drop

The friendliest trail in Val di Sole. Machine-shaped berms, gentle gradients, and smooth surfaces make this the ideal confidence builder before stepping up to the natural terrain.

Ponale Panorama

Intermediate

6.2km • 500m vertical drop

A scenic descent with panoramic views of the Trentino mountains. Rolling terrain, natural features, and alpine meadows make this a beautiful ride that rewards patience over aggression.

GETTING THERE

Nearest Airport

Verona Airport (VRN) -- 2 hour drive. Innsbruck Airport (INN) -- 2.5 hour drive.

By Car

A22 Brenner motorway, exit at San Michele all'Adige, then follow signs to Val di Sole. Ample parking at Daolasa gondola.

Public Transport

Local bus from Trento to Commezzadura. Summer shuttle services run between villages and the bike park lifts.

PRO TIPS

1

Bring Spare Brake Pads

The roots destroy brake pads at an alarming rate, especially in wet conditions. Pack at least one extra set -- you'll thank yourself by day two.

2

Check Weather Obsessively

Conditions change fast in the Trentino mountains. A dry morning can become a mud bath by noon. Monitor forecasts hourly and plan your runs accordingly.

3

Ride Daolasa First

Build confidence on the Daolasa Flow trail before attempting the Black Snake. The flow trail will calibrate your feel for the terrain without the consequences.

4

Try the Local Cuisine

Trentino cuisine is exceptional. After a day of fighting roots, reward yourself with local polenta, strangolapreti, and a glass of Teroldego. You've earned it.
