Fort William is hallowed ground. The Nevis Range track has hosted more World Cup rounds than any other venue, and its reputation is unmatched. The 2.8km course drops from the shoulder of Aonach Mor through exposed moorland, rock slabs, and into dense forest.

Rain is almost guaranteed -- this is the Scottish Highlands. The track is raw, natural, and brutally honest. No machine-shaped berms here. Fort William separates riders who can race from riders who can win. Every feature demands respect, and the mountain gives nothing away for free.

Beyond the World Cup track, the Nevis Range offers trails for all abilities, and the gondola ride alone is worth the trip for the views over Ben Nevis and the Great Glen. But make no mistake -- people come here for the DH track. It's a pilgrimage, and every downhill rider should make it at least once.

TRAIL HIGHLIGHTS

World Cup DH

Expert

2.8km • 555m vertical drop

The most iconic downhill track in the world. Exposed moorland, massive rock slabs, root-laced forest, and a finish arena that has witnessed decades of World Cup history. Raw and unforgiving.

Witches Trail

Advanced

3.5km • 400m vertical drop

A classic Scottish trail through ancient forest. Technical rock features, natural drops, and tight turns between the trees. Atmospheric riding through some of the Highlands' oldest woodland.

Top Chief

Intermediate

2.2km • 300m vertical drop

The most accessible trail on the mountain. Well-graded with manageable features, perfect for building confidence in Highland conditions before stepping up to the more demanding lines.

Rik's Rocks

Expert

1.8km • 350m vertical drop

A rock-garden gauntlet that tests technical skill and nerve in equal measure. Massive slabs, tight chutes, and committed lines that demand precise bike placement at speed.

GETTING THERE

Nearest Airport

Inverness Airport (INV) -- 1.5 hour drive. Glasgow Airport (GLA) -- 3 hour drive.

By Car

A82 scenic route from Glasgow through Glencoe -- one of the most beautiful drives in the UK. Nevis Range is signposted from Fort William town.

Public Transport

ScotRail to Fort William station. The West Highland Line from Glasgow is one of the world's great rail journeys. Local bus to Nevis Range.

PRO TIPS

1

Bring Waterproofs

It rains 250 days a year in Fort William. Waterproof jacket, mudguards, and goggles with clear lenses are non-negotiable. Embrace the wet -- it's part of the experience.

2

Ride the Gondola for the View

Even if you're not riding, take the gondola up for the panoramic views over Ben Nevis, the Great Glen, and on a clear day, the Inner Hebrides. Stunning.

3

Walk the Motorway Section

The motorway section of the World Cup track is fast and committing. Walk it before riding to identify the lines, the braking points, and the features that demand respect.

4

Visit the Ben Nevis Inn

The Ben Nevis Inn at the foot of the mountain is the perfect post-ride spot. Hearty Scottish food, local ales, and a crowd of muddy riders swapping war stories.
