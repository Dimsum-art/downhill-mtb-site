Verbier offers what few resorts can -- descents that start above 3,000 meters. The Mont Fort descent is a 12km journey from glacial terrain through alpine meadows and into the valley, dropping over 1,500 vertical meters in a single run. The air is thin, the views are staggering, and the trails are long.

Verbier is part of the massive 4 Vallees ski area, and in summer it transforms into a gravity playground. The Bike Park Verbier offers machine-shaped trails alongside raw natural singletrack. This is high-alpine riding at its finest.

"Starting a descent at 3,000 meters changes your perspective -- on riding, and on everything else."

TRAIL HIGHLIGHTS

Mont Fort Descent

Advanced

12km • 1,500m vertical drop

The ultimate high-alpine descent. Starting from glacial terrain above 3,000 meters, this epic 12km run drops through every type of mountain landscape before delivering you to the valley floor.

La Chaux Express

Intermediate

4.5km • 600m vertical drop

A flowing intermediate trail serviced by the La Chaux Express lift. Wide berms, gentle gradients, and stunning views of the Grand Combin massif make this a perfect warm-up run.

Tortin Enduro

Expert

6.8km • 900m vertical drop

A savage enduro descent through the wild Tortin valley. Loose scree, exposed traverses, and relentless technical sections make this one of the most demanding trails in the region.

Ruinettes Flow

Intermediate

3.2km • 450m vertical drop

A purpose-built flow trail from the Ruinettes mid-station. Smooth berms, tabletops, and rollers designed for speed and style. The ideal trail for building confidence at altitude.

GETTING THERE

Nearest Airport

Geneva International (GVA) -- 2 hour drive. Sion airport -- 30 minutes (limited flights).

By Car

A9 motorway to Martigny, then follow signs to Verbier via Le Chable. Well-maintained mountain road.

Public Transport

PostBus from Le Chable train station. Le Chable has direct rail connections from Martigny on the main Swiss rail network.

PRO TIPS

1

Acclimatize

Altitude affects performance above 2,500m. Take it easy on your first day and stay hydrated. The thin air will catch up with you faster than you expect.

2

Take the Mont Fort Cable Car Early

The summit trails are best ridden first thing in the morning. Empty trails, cooler temperatures, and the best light for those glacier views.

3

Bring Layers

Temperature drops dramatically with altitude. It can be 25C in the village and near freezing at the summit. Pack a windproof jacket and arm warmers.

4

Budget Accordingly

Switzerland is expensive. Lift passes, food, and accommodation all come at a premium. Book early and consider self-catering to manage costs.
