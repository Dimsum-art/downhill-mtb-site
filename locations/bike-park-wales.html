Bike Park Wales proved you don't need the Alps to build world-class trails. Carved from a Welsh hillside above Merthyr Tydfil, BPW has become the UK's premier uplift-served bike park. With 40+ trails ranging from gentle blue runs to terrifying black diamond lines, it's the perfect introduction to gravity riding.

The trails are machine-shaped and immaculately maintained, offering consistent quality rain or shine. The shuttle service means maximum descending with minimum effort.

Year-round operation and proximity to major cities make it incredibly accessible.

"No mountains? No problem. Wales proved that great trails are built, not born."

TRAIL HIGHLIGHTS

Rim Dinger

Expert

1.8km • 280m vertical drop

BPW's most feared trail. Steep, rocky, and unforgiving -- the name says it all. Technical rock gardens, exposed roots, and drops that demand full commitment.

Terry's Belly

Advanced

2.2km • 260m vertical drop

A red-graded classic that mixes technical sections with flow. Tight turns through dense forest, off-camber roots, and enough speed to keep advanced riders honest.

Melted Welly

Intermediate

1.5km • 200m vertical drop

A brilliant blue trail that flows beautifully. Machine-built berms, gentle jumps, and wide lines make this the perfect confidence builder for progressing riders.

Norkle

Intermediate

2.0km • 220m vertical drop

One of BPW's longest blues. Rollers, berms, and optional features throughout. A trail you can ride ten times and find a new line every run.

GETTING THERE

Nearest Airports

Cardiff Airport (CWL) -- 45 minute drive. Bristol Airport (BRS) -- 1 hour drive.

By Car

M4 motorway to A470 north. Well-signposted from Merthyr Tydfil. Ample free parking on site.

Public Transport

Train to Merthyr Tydfil station, then a short taxi ride to the park. Direct trains from Cardiff Central run regularly.

PRO TIPS

1

Book Shuttle Tickets Early

Book shuttle tickets in advance -- weekends sell out. Midweek sessions are quieter and often discounted, giving you more runs for your money.

2

Start on Blue Trails

Start on blue trails to learn the soil. Welsh dirt rides differently to alpine terrain -- it's softer, grippier when dry, and slippery when wet. Get a feel for it first.

3

Bring Mudguards

Bring mudguards -- Welsh rain is legendary. Even on dry days the forest trails can hold moisture. A front mudguard will save your face and your goggles.

4

Hit the Cafe

The on-site cafe does excellent post-ride food. Refuel with a proper Welsh breakfast or a loaded burger before tackling the afternoon session.
