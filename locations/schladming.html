Schladming's Planai mountain delivers one of the most relentless vertical descents in Europe. The 4.5km World Cup track drops 1,000 meters of pure vertical -- your forearms will be screaming by the bottom. The Bikepark Planai has expanded significantly, offering everything from beginner pump tracks to the infamous World Cup course.

The Schladming-Dachstein region provides stunning Styrian Alps scenery. The town is compact, bike-friendly, and has excellent infrastructure. This is Austrian efficiency applied to downhill mountain biking.

"1,000 meters of vertical in a single run. Your forearms will remember Schladming long after you leave."

TRAIL HIGHLIGHTS

Planai DH World Cup

Expert

4.5km • 1,000m vertical drop

The full World Cup downhill course from summit to valley. 1,000 meters of unrelenting vertical with rock gardens, root sections, and high-speed compressions that test even the best riders.

Flow One

Intermediate

3.8km • 600m vertical drop

A beautifully shaped flow trail with smooth berms, gentle jumps, and rollers. The perfect introduction to Schladming's terrain and an ideal warm-up before tackling the World Cup track.

Uphill Flow Trail

Advanced

5.2km • 700m vertical drop

A longer, more technical alternative descent with natural terrain features. Rocky sections, off-camber turns, and steeper pitches provide a serious challenge without the full World Cup intensity.

Hopsiland Kids Trail

Intermediate

1.5km • 200m vertical drop

A family-friendly trail with gentle gradients, small features, and fun elements designed for younger riders and beginners finding their feet on mountain terrain.

GETTING THERE

Nearest Airport

Salzburg Airport (SZG) -- 1.5 hour drive. Graz Airport (GRZ) -- 2 hour drive.

By Car

A10 motorway from Salzburg, exit Schladming. Well-signposted and easy to navigate. Ample parking at the Planai gondola base.

Public Transport

Direct train connections from Vienna and Salzburg to Schladming station. The gondola base is a short walk from the station.

PRO TIPS

1

Warm Up on Flow One

Don't go straight for the World Cup track. A couple of runs on Flow One will get your body and bike dialed in before you commit to the full 1,000m descent.

2

Summit Restaurant Views

Take the gondola to the summit restaurant for panoramic views of the Dachstein glacier. The food is solid Austrian fare and the views are worth the pause between runs.

3

Watch Your Brakes

Your brakes will overheat on the long descent. Let them cool between runs, and consider metallic pads if you're doing multiple laps. The vertical is relentless on brake systems.

4

Combine with Leogang

Leogang is just a 2 hour drive away. Combine both destinations for the ultimate Austrian downhill road trip -- two World Cup venues in one trip.
